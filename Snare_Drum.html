Snare Drum

We do not know who created the Snare Drum but we know it dates back to medieval times around 1300.



Its ancestor is the Tabor which was used by the fife and drum corps.



Tabor is a double headed instrument worn over the right shoulder by a strap.



A snare drum is known for being played with a wooden stick and has 3 ranges of sound from low to medium to high pitched.



The snare is played in orchestra, marching/concert band, drumlines, and drum corps.
